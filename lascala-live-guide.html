① What Is LaScala LIVE?
Same Game.
Bigger Stakes.

LaScala LIVE takes the core LaScala ladder game and opens it up to a crowd. One person is the Shooter — they climb the ladder, they throw the dice. Everyone else is the Crowd — they watch, they bet, they win or lose chips based on what happens.

You never have to shoot if you don't want to. Being in the crowd is its own game — reading the odds, backing your instincts, watching the momentum shift on every single throw.

② The Two Roles
Shooter
or Crowd?
🎲 The Shooter

You climb the ladder. You throw the dice. The crowd bets on you. Choose your start position — high for a fast game, low for drama. Every throw is yours. The tension is all on you. Win by landing exactly on top. Lose by rolling a double at any point.

👥 The Crowd

You watch. You bet. You don't touch the dice. Before each throw a timer counts down — place your chips on Make It, Don't Make It, or the likely diff range. When the dice fall, your bets resolve immediately. A betting timer auto-advances if you don't bet in time.

③ The Zones
Reading the
Ladder

The position number on the ladder tells you how many steps the shooter is from the top. That number maps to a Zone — and the Zone tells you instantly what kind of game you're in and what the odds look like.

🎯 ZONE
1–5 steps away. Shooter can win in one throw. Make It odds shortest. Maximum drama.
⚡ SHORT
6–8 steps. Close. Make It pays 2.1–2.5x. Good time to back the shooter.
🎲 CLASSIC
9–11 steps. Balanced game. Make It pays 2.6–3.1x. Both sides competitive.
🪜 LONG
12–14 steps. Shooter is underdog. Make It pays 3.3–3.9x. Don't Make It is favourite.
🌌 EPIC
15+ steps. Long game. Make It from 4.2x up to 12x+. Don't Make It near evens. Hedge window is wide open.
The Crowd Tip

Watch the Zone display on the ladder. When the shooter drops into Zone territory the crowd sentiment bar usually swings hard toward Make It — everyone gets excited. That's often exactly when the smart money quietly backs Don't Make It at lengthening odds. The double doesn't care how close they are.

⑥ Step by Step
How a

Shooter or Crowd. Choose your ladder length. Join the game.

A countdown timer gives you time to place your chips. Click Make It, Don't Make It, Low, or High. You can bet multiple times on different outcomes. If you're crowd, the timer auto-throws when it reaches zero.

Shooter throws (or the timer auto-throws for crowd play). Watch the dice animate. The difference moves the shooter on the ladder.

Position resets. Leaderboard updates. New betting window opens. Keep going.

⑦ Tips
Playing
Smart
When should I bet Make It?
Early, when the shooter is far from the top and the odds are long. If they make it, you've got a big return. If they don't, you haven't bet much. The odds shorten as they get closer — so early Make It bets are high risk, high reward.
When should I bet Don't Make It?
When the shooter is near the top but not there yet. That's when the odds on Don't Make It are at their longest — because there's still a double lurking. One bad throw and the crowd's hopes are gone.
What are Low and High diff bets?
These are bets on the next throw only. In the crowd game they're grouped for simplicity — Low covers diff 1 (odds 2.4/1) and diff 2 (odds 3.3/1), High covers diff 3 (4.7/1), diff 4 (7.5/1), and diff 5 (16.1/1). All odds are mathematically verified at a 5% target margin. Both types pay immediately regardless of the overall game outcome — good for action on every throw even when you're unsure about the final result.
Can I bet both Make It and Don't Make It?
Yes. And if you time it right — betting one early at long odds and the other later when those odds have lengthened — you can guarantee a return regardless of outcome. This is the LaScala Hedge. Advanced play. Very satisfying when it works.
What happens if I run out of chips?
Start a new game to reset your balance back to 1,000 virtual chips. Your Prize Cards are saved and won't be lost. Only your chip balance resets.

② Card Details
Every Card
Is Different

Each card has three stats: a Power rating (the big number at the bottom), a Special stat unique to that card, and a Tier that determines its rarity and collectability.

Cards currently serve as collectables and achievement markers — proof of your win streaks. In a future version, card Power ratings will feed into a Top Trumps-style head-to-head game against other collectors.

Cards Are Saved Automatically

Your card collection is saved to your browser. You won't lose it when you close the tab. To transfer cards to a new device or trade with others, a shareable card code system is coming in the next version.

Future: Card Trading

Version 2 of LaScala LIVE will introduce unique card codes — a string you can copy and share. Send your code to another player; they enter it and add your card to their collection. Rare and Legendary cards with low print runs will have genuine collector scarcity. Cards earned early — before the player base grows — will be the rarest.

③ The Collection Game
Why Cards
Matter

Prize Cards borrow from the same psychology that makes Pokémon, Panini stickers, and Top Trumps addictive: the desire to complete a set. Once you have two common cards, you want all four. Once you have a rare, you want an epic.

The streak mechanic is deliberately hard. Winning 7 in a row to earn a Legendary card is genuinely difficult — every round has a real chance of ending in a double. That rarity is earned, not given. Players who have a Legendary card deserve to show it off.

In the roadmap, card Power ratings will unlock exclusive game features, bonus starting positions, and early access to new game variants. Cards become your LaScala passport.

① What It Is
The Product
Overview

LaScala LIVE is a browser-based crowd betting game built on the LaScala game mechanic. It operates entirely in virtual chips — no real money changes hands in this version. It is a social gaming and audience engagement product, legally equivalent to a free-to-play game with a virtual economy.

Legal Status — Current Version

LaScala LIVE v1 uses virtual chips only. No real money is wagered, won, or lost. Under UK and most international law, virtual chip games do not constitute gambling and do not require a gambling licence. This is the same legal basis as social casino games (Zynga Poker, House of Fun, etc.).

Odds & Margin — Background

The virtual odds in LaScala LIVE are based on mathematically verified probabilities (AIQ Consulting Limited, 10 million trial simulation). All throw bets are priced at a 5% target margin. Make It / Don't Make It odds update by position using the verified probability table. When the real-money version launches, these same verified odds feed directly into the licensed product paytable — no additional mathematical work required.

Important: Real Money Version

Any future version incorporating real money stakes requires a full gambling licence from the relevant jurisdiction (e.g. UK Gambling Commission). Do not introduce real money mechanics without obtaining appropriate legal and regulatory clearance first.

③ The Crowd Mechanic
How the
Crowd Works

In the current version, the "crowd" is simulated for single-player use. When a player joins as Crowd, they see a fake leaderboard of named players and a crowd sentiment bar — these are generated randomly to create the feeling of a live game.

The real multiplayer version — where actual different people see the same game simultaneously — requires a lightweight server to synchronise game state. This is Version 2 and is described in the Roadmap tab.

🎲
Shooter Role
One player controls the dice. They throw at their own pace. Crowd has a timer that auto-advances if they don't bet — keeping the game moving regardless.
👥
Crowd Role
Player watches and bets. An 8-second countdown runs before each throw. If crowd bets before timer expires, their bet is placed. Timer auto-throws after countdown regardless.
📊
Sentiment Bar
Shows simulated crowd split between Make It and Don't Make It. Updates when player bets. In real multiplayer, reflects actual vote distribution.
🏆
Leaderboard
Populated with simulated players in v1. In multiplayer, reflects real player chip rankings. Player's own position always highlighted.
