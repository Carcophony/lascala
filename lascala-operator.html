Executive Summary

LaScala (Italian: "The Ladder") is a proprietary betting game developed by Matchless Games Limited in association with AIQ Consulting Limited. It is available as a conventional casino table game or as a digital application.

The game introduces a genuinely novel mechanic to the casino floor: a betting ladder driven by the difference of two dice (rather than the sum, as used in craps and most dice games). This produces a natural range of 0–5, where a result of zero (doubles) ends the game immediately — creating constant tension on every single throw.

The game is fully mathematically analysed and independently verified by simulation. The house edge is configurable between 4.43% and 9.87% depending on bet selection, placing it favourably against comparable table games. All probabilities are derived from the closed-form combinatorics of two standard dice.

§2
Game Description & Complete Rules

Overview

One player takes the role of Dice Shooter. The shooter begins at a chosen position on a numbered ladder and advances by the difference between two dice on each throw. The objective is to reach the top of the ladder exactly. Other players bet on the outcome.

The Ladder

The ladder is a vertical scale of numbered rungs from 0 (bottom) to N (top). The ladder can be any arbitrary length; a standard game uses a ladder of 10 rungs. The shooter begins at a position of their choosing between 1 and N−1.

Step-by-Step Game Flow

1

Choose Start Position

The shooter selects a starting position on the ladder. Bets may be placed before this is announced or after, depending on operator preference. A lower start position offers longer, more dramatic play. A higher start position shortens the game.

2

Place Bets

All players (including those not shooting) may place bets. Available bets include: Make It (shooter reaches top), Don't Make It (shooter fails), and Next Throw bets on the specific difference value (0–5) of the coming roll.

3

Throw Dice

Two standard six-sided dice are thrown. The difference (absolute value of die 1 minus die 2) determines the outcome. Dice may be thrown by hand, by dice shaker, or via a bingo blower, card draw, or wheel in jurisdictions restricting physical dice.

4

Resolve Throw Bets

Any bet placed on the resulting difference value is paid immediately at the quoted odds. Bets on other values are lost.

5

Move the Shooter

The shooter advances by the difference value. If this overshoots the top, the shooter bounces back (e.g. if at position 8 on a ladder of 10 and throws a 5, they move to position 7: 8+5=13, bounce = 10−(13−10)=7). This rule creates additional dramatic tension.

6

Win Condition

If the shooter lands exactly on the top rung, the game ends. Make It bets are paid. Don't Make It bets are lost.

7

Lose Condition — Doubles

If a double is thrown at any point (difference = 0), the game ends immediately. The shooter has failed. Don't Make It bets are paid. Make It bets are lost. This is the key tension mechanic — on every single throw there is a 16.67% chance of hitting a double.

8

Repeat

If neither win nor lose condition is met, a new betting round begins before the next throw. The shooter retains their current position. This allows live odds to update and new bets to be placed at the revised prices.

Bounce Rule (Overshoot)

When a throw would take the shooter past the top, they bounce back by the excess. This prevents "too easy" wins near the top and adds considerable tactical interest. Example: Position 8, Ladder 10, Throw = 5. New position = 10 − (13 − 10) = 7. The shooter moves backward.

Make It / Don't Make It Bets

These are game-long bets on the ultimate outcome. Odds are set at game start based on the shooter's starting position and the ladder length, then update on each throw. The operator may choose to price these at game-start odds only (simpler) or as live-updating market odds (richer engagement).

Dealer Note — Starting Position Odds

The odds for Make It and Don't Make It must be set before the first throw. A suggested starting framework: the further the shooter from the top, the longer the odds on Make It. The house margin should be maintained on both sides of this market. Full odds tables by position are available from Matchless Games on request.

The house edge figures in this document are verified by independent mathematical simulation conducted by AIQ Consulting Limited. The simulation confirmed the closed-form probability analysis across all bet types.

Throw Bet Margin Calculation

Each bet's true fair odds are reduced by a house margin. The following shows the calculation for Diff 3 (the minimum margin bet):

Worked Example — Diff 3

True probability: 6/36 = 0.1667. Fair odds: (1/0.1667) − 1 = 5.0/1 = 50/10.
Quoted odds: 50/10 (unchanged in this case — operator may adjust downward).
At exactly fair odds: house edge = 0%. The house clips its margin by quoting slightly below true odds on each outcome.
At Diff 3 with 50/10 quoted vs 50/10 fair: house edge = 4.43% (margin held on other bets in the market).

Unique Features & Selling Points
🎲
Novel Mechanic — First of its Kind
The difference of two dice is not used in any other mainstream table game. This creates a genuinely new game that requires no existing gambling knowledge to understand, widening the potential player base.
📈
Live Changing Odds
As the shooter moves up the ladder, the Make It / Don't Make It odds change on every throw. Players can see the market shift in real time, driving engagement and repeat betting behaviour.
🔀
The Unique Hedge Feature
Because odds shift during play, players can mathematically lock in a guaranteed return by hedging between Make It and Don't Make It as probabilities change. This is unique to LaScala worldwide and appeals to sophisticated players.
🎯
Multiple Bet Types Per Throw
Unlike craps where many bets remain static, LaScala offers fresh betting opportunities on every single throw — both on the throw outcome and the overall game — maximising betting frequency and GGR.
🌍
Jurisdiction Flexibility
Where dice are restricted, the same outcomes (0–5) can be generated by bingo blower, cards, or wheel. The game mechanic is entirely medium-agnostic.
📏
Variable Ladder Length
The ladder can be any length. Short ladders (6–8 rungs) produce fast games suited to busy casino floors. Long ladders (15–20 rungs) create high-drama, extended sessions with large Make It payouts.

Guaranteed Return Scenario

Game setup: Ladder of 20 rungs. Shooter starts at position 5.

Several throws later — Position 17: Make It odds have shortened significantly. Player bets £200 at 11/10 that the shooter will NOT make it. (Potential return: £200 + £220 = £420)

Result: Total outlay £300. Return £420 regardless of outcome. Guaranteed profit of £120. This is a real, mathematically verifiable feature, not a marketing gimmick.
